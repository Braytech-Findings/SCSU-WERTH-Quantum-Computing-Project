# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

This project welcomes respectful participation from students, researchers, educators, software contributors, and readers at every experience level.

Constructive participation includes:

- explaining technical ideas without belittling beginners;
- criticizing methods or claims rather than people;
- separating evidence from speculation;
- acknowledging uncertainty and correcting mistakes openly;
- respecting authorship, citations, privacy, and confidentiality; and
- helping keep discussions focused, reproducible, and scientifically honest.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, discrimination, personal attacks, deliberate misinformation, credential exposure, confidential-data disclosure, or pressure to overstate research findings.

## Research discussions

Disagreement is normal and useful. When challenging a result, identify the relevant data, method, assumption, or interpretation. Avoid presenting proxy outputs as physical measurements or treating emulator evidence as physical-QPU evidence.

## Enforcement

Repository maintainers may edit, hide, lock, or remove comments, issues, pull requests, or contributions that violate these standards. Repeated or serious misconduct may result in temporary or permanent participation restrictions.

Sensitive conduct or security concerns should be reported privately using the contact information in [CITATION.cff](CITATION.cff), not through a public issue.
